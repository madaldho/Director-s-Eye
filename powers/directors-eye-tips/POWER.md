# Director's Eye Tips

## Overview

Quick reference guide for getting the best results from Director's Eye AI cinematography analysis. Learn what makes a great shot and how to improve your scores.

## Scoring Breakdown

Director's Eye evaluates images on three key dimensions:

- **Lighting (1-10)**: Quality, direction, and mood of light
- **Composition (1-10)**: Rule of thirds, leading lines, framing
- **Mood (1-10)**: Emotional impact and storytelling

## Quick Tips for Better Scores

### Lighting
- Use golden hour for warm, cinematic tones
- Avoid harsh midday shadows
- Side lighting adds depth and dimension

### Composition
- Apply rule of thirds for balanced framing
- Use leading lines to guide the viewer's eye
- Leave breathing room around subjects

### Mood
- Consider color grading for emotional impact
- Match lighting to the story you're telling
- Contrast creates visual tension

## Common Issues

**Low lighting score?**
- Check for flat, even lighting - add contrast
- Look for distracting shadows

**Low composition score?**
- Subject may be centered - try off-center placement
- Check for cluttered backgrounds

**Low mood score?**
- Colors may be too neutral - consider grading
- Story intent may be unclear
